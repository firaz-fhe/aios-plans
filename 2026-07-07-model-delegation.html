the model delegation system

fable-5 stops doing everything itself. it becomes the director — planning, judging, and shipping — while cheaper models do the heavy lifting. same output quality, a fraction of the usage.

tl;dr — we teach every aios surface (terminal, discord, superapp, subagents) a routing table: fable-5 orchestrates on high effort, gpt-5.5 (codex — basically free on your sub) does bulk/token-heavy work, opus-4-8 handles taste-sensitive middleweight tasks. rules live in CLAUDE.md, mechanics live in 3 skills, guardrails watch usage. expected: 50–70% usage cut, fewer session-limit lockouts like tonight's.

02the delegation rules

  • bulk + token-heavy → codex: reading huge logs, giant pdfs, spreadsheets, codebase archaeology, clear-spec implementations, migrations
  • second-perspective reviews → codex: independent review of diffs/plans before shipping — different model family = different blind spots
  • anything that ships to a human → taste ≥ 8: fable or opus writes/edits final ui, copy, api surfaces
  • escalate freely: if a cheap model's output misses the bar, redo with a smarter one without asking — escalating costs less than shipping mediocre work
  • inside workflows: claude-only models can be workflow agents, so a sonnet(low) wrapper spawns codex, collects results, reports back — labeled with a [5.5] prefix so we can see delegation live
